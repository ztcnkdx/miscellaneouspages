▼ Methodology: parameter definitions, encodings, calculations, and sources

Schema redesign rationale

v2's single s status field collapsed orthogonal dimensions into one ordinal, causing the reviewer-flagged class of errors where D (decriminalized) overwrote M (medical) when both were active. v3 stores four independent booleans (med, dec, au, ret) and derives a 5-level display label only for visualization. Tax fields are partially normalized into retail purchaser excise (te), state sales (ts), local max (tl), wholesale/cultivation/upstream (tw), potency-tax flag (tp), structure type (tt), display heuristic (tf), plus channel-specific overrides where present (te_med, ts_med); channel-specific fields override shared scalars where populated. Forms are six booleans; possession splits into mp/dp/ap for medical/decrim/adult-use channels; home grow splits into medical vs adult-use plant counts (with explicit warning that the scalars are not empirical-grade — see #7-8).

1-4. Status booleans (med, dec, au, ret)

med = patient-side medical-cannabis program legally enacted (legal protection convention; does NOT require licensed access to be operational; e.g., NE 2024-12-10 onward where Init 437/438 grants protection but licensed access is not yet active by Apr 2026); dec = nonmedical possession decriminalized below criminal threshold; au = adult-use possession legally protected (irrespective of retail); ret = adult-use state-licensed retail cannabis sales actively occurring (NOT medical-dispensary retail; medical retail is implied by med:1 plus operational program but is not separately tracked). For NE-style legal-protection-without-access, med:1, ret:0, win:"...licensed access NOT operational...". The composite "Legal Status" tab displays a 5-level summary: P(prohibited) → D(decrim only, no medical) → M(medical legal, irrespective of decrim) → AN(adult-use possession, no retail) → A(adult-use w/retail). Decriminalization does not demote states with active medical programs.

5-6. Possession (medical / decrim / adult-use) - now atomic

v3 round-10: ap is now strictly adult-use. Three independent possession fields: med_poss (mp) = patient possession ceiling; dp = decriminalized civil-fine threshold (populated only when dec:1 + au:0); ap = adult-use lawful possession (populated only when au:1). When au:1 transitions occur, dp is set to "NA". When dec:1 + au:0 events occur, ap is "NA" and dp holds the threshold. -1 = criminal (any-amount possession remains a criminal offense; e.g., NC, prohibition states). The "Decrim Threshold" tab renders dp; the "Adult-Use Possession" tab renders ap; both are first-class parameters.

7-8. Home Grow (medical / adult-use)

WARNING: mh and ah are coarse single-scalar plant counts and SHOULD NOT be used as empirical variables. The encoding is internally inconsistent across states because no single semantics fits all jurisdictions: in ME 2018-2023 baseline ah:3 = mature plants only (3 mature + 12 immature + unlimited seedlings, raised to 6 mature on 2023-10-25 by PL 2023 c.220); in CT ah:6 conflates 3 mature + 3 immature; in NM ah:6 conflates 6 mature + 6 immature with a separate 12-mature household cap; in CO ah:6 = 6 plants per person with 3-mature limit and 12-plant household cap. Treating these as comparable scalars produces misleading cross-state comparisons. Empirical work must recode with separate fields: *_mature, *_immature, *_seedlings, *_residence_cap, *_per_patient_or_adult, and *_hardship_or_distance_conditioned. The composite "Home Grow" tab displays max(mh, ah) for visual cross-state ordering only and is not a publication-ready variable.

9. Flower THC Cap (% w/w)

Maximum THC concentration permitted in cannabis flower. "NA" = flower-form cannabis is not permitted as a product (GA, IA, AL, KS, ID, etc.). null = flower permitted with no statutory potency cap. Numeric cap: CT 30%, MS 30%, VT 30%, KY 35% (corrected from null), TX historical 0.5-1% before HB 46.

10. Concentrate THC Cap (% w/w)

Cap on THC concentration in extracts/concentrates. "NA" = concentrates not authorized as cannabis product (GA's program is low-THC oil only). CT 60% pre-2025-10-01, raised to 70% by PA 25-166 with prefilled cartridges for use in an electronic cannabis delivery system exempt from the 70% non-flower cap (statutory phrase from PA 25-166 / HB 7181 enrolled; secondary "vapes exempt" characterizations are paraphrases of this narrower carve-out); MS 60%; VT 60% (vape exempt); KY 70%; OH 70% from 2026-03-20 (SB 56). Most adult-use states have no concentration cap. MA does NOT have a general 70% concentrate potency cap — its constraint is purchase equivalency under 935 CMR 500.140 (1 oz flower = 5 g active THC concentrate pre-2026; 2 oz = 10 g post-2026), captured in txc rather than cc.

11. Edible Per-Serving Cap (mg THC)

THC per individually-dosed serving. Industry-modal 10 mg; CT/VT/MS 5 mg; AK 5 mg; MA 5.5 mg with regulatory variance (encoded as 5 with note); HI 10 mg medical; RI 10 mg adult-use. Adult-use values; medical caps shown separately where relevant.

12. Edible Per-Package Cap (mg THC)

Total THC per retail package. Modal 100 mg adult-use; FL medical 200 mg; HI medical 1000 mg (corrected from 100); MN adult-use 200 mg (corrected from 50); DE 50 mg (corrected from 100); MN hemp 50 mg (separate channel); RI 100 mg.

13-18. Form Booleans (flower / smoke / vape / conc / edible / low-THC oil)

Atomic boolean flags replacing v2's ordinal frm. flower_ok = raw plant material permitted as a product; smoke_ok = combustion permitted (false in UT, KY, WV, PA which authorize plant material only via vaporization); vape_ok = vaporization permitted; conc_ok = concentrate/extract products permitted; edible_ok = traditional edible products permitted; low_thc_oil = state has a separate low-THC oil program (true in GA at ≤5%; some narrow CBD states). Note: Minnesota medical edibles (gummies/chews) authorized 2022-08-01 at 10mg/100mg; Minnesota adult-use cannabis edibles authorized 2023-08-01 at 10mg/200mg; Minnesota hemp-derived edibles are a separate channel. Florida edibles authorized 2020-08-27 (10mg/200mg medical). Pennsylvania has not authorized edibles throughout the window. Composite "Form Profile" tab summarizes.

19. State Cannabis Excise Tax (%) - retail-purchaser excise only

State-imposed cannabis-specific excise rate as a percentage of retail price imposed on the purchaser. Excludes state sales/use tax, local taxes, AND upstream/wholesale gross-receipts taxes (which appear in tw with tt:"upstream" or "upstream_gross_receipts"). 0% when no retail-purchaser excise (medical-only states with sales-tax exemption; or states like PA where the cannabis-specific tax is an upstream grower/processor gross-receipts tax not imposed on retail purchasers; or IL medical, where the 7% cultivation privilege tax is on cultivators not retail). Examples: KY 0% (medical exempt); MN 10% pre-2025-07-01 then 15%; CA 15% → 19% (2025-07-01) → 15% (2025-10-01); WA 37%; NY AU 9% retail + 9% wholesale (see tt:retail_plus_wholesale). PA 5% is NOT a retail excise and appears under tw with tt:"upstream"; WV 10% is downstream dispensary gross receipts (tt:"dispensary_gr").

20. State Sales Tax on Cannabis (%)

Standard state sales/use tax applied to cannabis sales (where applicable). Medical cannabis is sales-tax exempt in CA, FL, NY (medical), MD (medical), NJ (medical post-2022-07-01 phaseout), PA, MN, and others. Note for NV: medical cannabis is exempt from the 10% retail cannabis excise but NOT from sales/use tax — Nevada medical sales remain subject to state sales tax (6.85%) plus local variability. Adult-use sales typically subject to standard state sales tax in addition to excise (except where AU is explicitly exempt: NY post-2024-06-01, NJ AU exempt from cannabis-specific tax but subject to standard sales tax). Medical-channel rate captured in ts_med where channel-specific.

21. Local cannabis tax (%) - representative single layer; not necessarily max

WARNING: tl is a single-scalar approximation with mixed semantics across states — it should be read as a representative local cannabis-tax layer, not as a uniform "maximum" across jurisdictions. Across rows, tl means different things: in some states it is the statutory maximum single local layer (e.g., NJ ≤2% Cannabis Transfer Tax; CT 3% municipal cap); in others it is the maximum single layer authorized by statute even though the combined stacked burden is higher (IL tl:3.75 reflects the max single layer, county unincorporated; the combined incorporated-area maximum is approximately 6% = 3% municipal + 3% county); and in CO the value is representative-Denver-special rather than statewide max — CO has no statewide statutory local-tax ceiling and Denver's special retail-marijuana tax of 5.5% is shown as the representative layer (Denver also imposes a separate ~5.15% general local tax; see CO tw for context). For empirical work, do not treat tl as comparable across states; recode with structured fields: tl_municipal_max, tl_county_unincorp_max, tl_county_incorp_max, tl_combined_incorp_max, tl_representative_special.

22. Wholesale / Cultivation Tax

String description of weight-based or wholesale-revenue cannabis tax not captured by the retail-percent fields. AK $50/oz mature bud cultivation; CO 15% wholesale at first sale; ME $335/lb flower; CA cultivation tax (repealed 2022-07-01, AB 195); MN gross-receipts tax (10% → 15%, 2025-07-01).

23. Potency-Based Tax (boolean)

True if state imposes a tax structured by THC concentration or per-mg-THC. NY pre-June 2024 had per-mg-THC (replaced 2024-06-01 with 9% wholesale); IL has tier-graduated (10/20/25%); CT has hybrid (potency excise + retail %). Important for the OM model because per-mg taxes are theoretically Pigouvian on dose.

24. Effective Estimated Tax % (composite, non-authoritative)

WARNING: tf is a display heuristic, NOT a clean numeric policy variable. It is intended only as a rough cross-state ordering for the headline visualization. Do not use tf for empirical regressions. The atomic component fields (te, ts, tl, tw, tp) are authoritative; tf approximates a state-only retail-equivalent burden but is applied inconsistently across states with tiered (IL: 10/20/25%), potency-based (NY 2022-2024), hybrid (CT), or weight-based (AK, ME) structures. For tiered states, tf reflects the high tier or a heuristic blend. Local taxes are excluded in principle but included in some jurisdictions where the local rate is fixed and broadly representative. For empirical work, recompute tf from the atomic fields with an explicit formula; for tiered states, use tf_min/tf_modal/tf_max derived from te.

25. Per-Trip Transaction Cap (oz/visit)

Single-transaction purchase ceiling, distinct from possession. CT launched 0.25 oz / visit then 0.5 oz from Dec 2023, raised again to 1 oz from 2025-07-30 (CT DCP notice); CO 1 oz/day flower (post HB 21-1317); IL has resident/non-resident differential (encoded via "sb" flag). NV raised to 2.5 oz from 2024-01-01. OH raised daily flower limit from 1 oz to 2.5 oz effective 2025-06-04 (DCC announcement; corroborated by Marijuana Moment / Cannabis Law Report / ABC6 — round-15 audit corrected the file from a prior 2025-06-06 mis-dating).

26. Concentrate cap / equivalent (g) - txc (mixed AU-transaction / AU-possession / medical-possession)

Concentrate ceiling expressed in grams. Mixed-channel field: covers adult-use retail transaction/possession equivalents AND medical-only concentrate possession quantities. Adult-use states (AK 7g, AZ 5g, CA 8g, CO 8g, CT 1.25g pre-2023-12-01 / 2.5g from 2023-12-01 / 5g from 2025-07-30, DE 12g, IL 5g, MD 12g, MA 5g pre-2026 / 10g post-2026, MI 15g, MO 24g, MT 8g, NJ 4g, NM 16g, NV 3.543g pre-2024 / 7.0875g from 2024, NY 24g, OH 15g, OR 10g, RI 5g, VT 5g, WA 7g). Medical-only states with explicit concentrate caps: OK 28.35g (1 oz medical concentrate possession). Pure prohibition states or states without explicit concentrate quantities: txc:0. For empirical work that requires channel-clean fields, split into txc_au and txc_med. Companion product-THC-equivalent fields (e.g., 750 mg edible product limit, 800 mg infused THC) are noted in tw or win.

27. Per-Window Aggregate Quota

Time-window aggregate THC ceiling per patient (the "blending-robust" instrument). Rendered as a categorical: present vs. absent. IA 4.5 g THC / 90 days; MS 3 oz / 30 days + 3.5 g daily MMCEU; FL 2.5 oz smokable / 35 days; AR 2.5 oz / 14 days; NM medical (currently 15 oz / 90 days); AL 50 mg / day. Most adult-use states do not impose aggregate windows.

28. Tax Structure Type

Categorical type of the tax architecture. Live categories used in v3 data: none (medical-exempt or no operational program); flat ad valorem retail (most adult-use states: AZ, CA, IL post-AU, MA pre-2026, MO, OR, RI baseline+post-2022, etc.; NJ is NOT flat — see sales_plus_weight below); grad graduated by potency (IL adult-use: 10%/20%/25% by THC tier); pot per-mg-THC tax (NY 2022-12-29 to 2024-06-01); hybrid per-mg potency excise + retail % (CT); weight weight-based cultivation tax (AK $/oz); upstream upstream grower/processor gross-receipts tax (PA 5%); dispensary_gr downstream dispensary gross-receipts tax (WV 10%); mixed upstream excise + retail sales tax (MS 5% cultivator + 7% retail sales); retail_plus_wholesale retail excise + separate wholesale tax (NY post-2024-06-01 9% retail + 9% wholesale; CO 15% retail + 15% wholesale; MI post-2026 24% wholesale + 10% retail; UT n/a); retail_plus_weight retail excise + weight-based excise (ME 2020-onward 10%/14% retail + $223/lb-style weight schedule; CA pre-2022 15% excise + $9.25/oz cultivation, becomes flat after 2022-07-01 cultivation-tax repeal); sales_plus_weight sales tax + per-ounce cultivator fee (NJ AU: 6.625% sales + up to 2% local + SEEF per-oz; SEEF 1.10→1.52→1.24→2.50). Theoretically distinct because consumer blending response differs across structures; tt classification is the headline machine-readable summary, atomic component fields (te, tw, etc.) are authoritative.

29. Cross-Border Special Instruments

Categorical: none, residency differential (IL 30 g/15 g resident/non-resident), distance-conditioned home grow (NV >25 mi from dispensary; AZ medical pre-2020), or both. Captures regulator's endogenous response to predicted cross-border arbitrage.

Geometry provenance (v4 round-17 reviewer-feedback addition). The STATE_PATHS object embedded in this file was generated by running audit/scripts/build_state_paths.js (Node.js) on 2026-04-28 against the GeoJSON above. The build script (a) clipped Alaska's Aleutian wrap-around longitudes (kept lon strictly between −180° and −129°), (b) projected the lower-48 polygons via cos-corrected linear lat/long → SVG with the source-coordinate bounding box (−125°,−66°,24.5°,49.5°) mapped into the SVG box (x: 30→920, y: 60→470) inside the 960×600 viewBox, (c) projected AK and HI separately into bottom-left and bottom-center inset boxes (each cos-corrected at the feature's own mid-latitude), (d) synthesized a small square marker for DC at the projected DC longitude/latitude (−77.04°,38.91°) with a leader line to its inset position, and (e) rounded every SVG coordinate to one decimal to keep the embedded path data compact (~40 KB total).
